A Grand Day Out

← Return to Index

My reputation spread, and I was instructed by a man facing a series of robberies in East Yorkshire. It was many months after ex parte Briggs No. 2 had been widely reported, but as I was to learn, the new CTL law had not seeped into Grimsby - a less than salubrious port town.

The case papers landed on the 66th day, with a request I agree to an extension of the CTL! On the 68th day, I set off for the two hour journey to Grimsby armed with coloured pie charts and a plethora of case law.

In the court room, I informed the prosecutor I was to oppose his request to extend the CTL. “But you have the papers, Mr Green.” I smiled, “I know”, and said no more awaiting the case to be called on.

The case was duly called and the same well-trodden path was stamped upon by the prosecutor. He referred to the six robberies and to the fact the offences could not be more grave – given they involved staff members being tied up. It was all fluff and prejudice, without a descent into “due expedition”.

By now I had further honed my address on CTLs. I was keen to point out to the Bench their brother colleagues in Leeds had been bold, but utterly correct, in applying CTL legislation. I had a twinkle in my eye as I walked them through ‘Briggs’ with Nicholas Green Solicitors emblazoned on the front of the rulings. This was no amateur advocate flying a kite. I stressed how concerned I was that, over six months since the divisional court ruling, those prosecuting were still failing in their duties. My pie charts were digested with alacrity.

Forty minutes later, the magistrates were back in court confirming the Crown had not acted with expedition and there would be no extension of the CTL. Bail with conditions was agreed and the prosecutor (with no police presence in court) failed to lodge an appeal. My client was released from custody.

I had the pleasure of the court usher sidling up to me outside court. He announced, in a gruff Yorkshire accent,

“That were grand, Mr Green – no one has tried that before in Grimsby.”

“What,” I laughed, “apply English law?”
