Decades ago, I travelled to the Lancastrian mill town of Accrington. Its bricks (the hardest and densest in the world) prop up Blackpool Tower and were used in the construction of the Empire State Building.

I was attending the Magistrates Court to represent a local youth who had been charged with murder. I note the court no longer exists as it was amalgamated with Blackburn Magistrates, its neighbour four miles to the East.

I had seen the punter in the cells and was proceeding to obtain instructions from his 17 year old pink-track suited girlfriend.

I was busy attempting to gather bail conditions I could offer to the Magistrates against the CPS’s strong argument bail was inappropriate.

At her feet was a clapped out stroller, from within which stared an unblinking, blue-eyed, feral child – no more than a year into its life on earth.

As I finished taking instructions I smiled at the baby, who proceeded to hold its arms out to me. Whilst arching his back against the restraints, he uttered what I believed was his first word…

‘Help.’

Has a baby ever assessed his plight in Accrington better than my murderous client’s son?
